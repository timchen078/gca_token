Risk Mistake 1

Position size too large

A $5,000 notional position on a $1,000 account leaves little room for volatility. The replay reduces the example exposure to a size where a planned stop does not dominate the entire account.

Risk Mistake 2

No invalidation rule

The sample requires an invalidation level before entry. If price reaches that level, the review says the trade idea is wrong and no new exposure should be added.

Risk Mistake 3

Funding and volatility ignored

Liquidation risk is not only direction. Funding cost, sudden spread changes, and exchange-side mark price movement all need to be included in the replay.

Safer Replay Template

Maximum account riskDefine a fixed loss budget before entry; this sample uses 1% as an education-only reference.
Position sizingSize from invalidation distance first, then check leverage, not the other way around.
Entry conditionOnly enter if the setup, invalidation level, and exit plan are written before execution.
Invalidation conditionIf the level is hit, close or stop reviewing the setup; do not average down by default.
Post-event reviewRecord what changed, whether the rule was followed, and what size would have survived normal volatility.
