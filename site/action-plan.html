Public Claim Boundaries

What Not To Say

These limits protect the project from misleading users and from creating avoidable review problems with wallets, explorers, and listing platforms.

  • Do not say the BaseScan token profile is approved until BaseScan publishes it.
  • Do not say GCA has a third-party audit until an independent audit report exists.
  • Do not describe current liquidity as deep or suitable for large trades.
  • Do not describe the owner reserve as locked, vested, or multisig-controlled unless that changes on-chain.
  • Do not promise price movement, returns, income, reimbursement, or token buybacks.
  • Do not describe holder credits, GCA Member status, or the 10,000 GCA member benefit as cash, trading permission, or automatic self-service claims.
  • Do not create fake trading activity or present internal test swaps as organic demand.
