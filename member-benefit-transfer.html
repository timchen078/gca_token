GCA Member Benefit Transfer Runbook

This page defines the manual operator controls for the planned one-time 10,000 GCA member benefit transfer after support review, 30-day holding-period verification, and member ledger approval.

The transfer is not automatic and is not self-service claimable. If approved, it must come from project or owner-held reserve on Base Mainnet; the GCA token contract has no mint function.

Manual Transfer Steps

1. Confirm Review Record

Confirm registrationId, walletAddress, holdingStartDate, evidenceTxHash, evidenceTxHashFormatOk, holdingPeriodDaysVerified, and eligible status.

2. Verify Current Balance

Run a fresh read-only Base Mainnet ERC-20 balanceOf check for the verified wallet before preparing a transfer.

3. Prepare Reserve Wallet

Use the project or owner-held reserve wallet on Base Mainnet. Do not request user secrets, custody, or fund transfers.

4. Send 10,000 GCA

Send exactly 10,000 GCA to the approved verified Base wallet. Confirm token contract and recipient before signing.

5. Record Transfer Hash

After confirmation, record memberBenefitTransferTx, transferredAt, transferredAmount, sourceWallet, recipientWallet, and reviewerNote.

6. Close Review

Set memberBenefitClaimStatus to transferred or closed only after the public transaction hash is recorded.

  • Custody request or fund-transfer request.

Public Claim Boundaries

  • Safe to say: GCA has published a manual transfer runbook for the planned one-time 10,000 GCA member benefit.
  • Safe to say: approval requires support review, member ledger approval, 30-day holding-period verification, and a fresh read-only balance check.
  • Do not say the 10,000 GCA member benefit is self-service claimable today.
  • Do not say holding 1,000,000 GCA automatically triggers a transfer.
  • Do not describe the member benefit as newly minted, cash, income, reimbursement, trading permission, voting control, lifetime access, price support, or risk-control bypass.
